Again, to revise linearity,
$f:\mathbb{R}^{n}\to \mathbb{R}^{n}$ is linear $\iff$ $\forall c_{1}, c_{2} \in \mathbb{R},\, \forall x, y \in \mathbb{R}^{n}: f(c_{1}x+c_{2}y) = c_{1}f(x) + c_{2}f(y)$

And, a theorem in Linear Algebra tells us $f$ is linear $\iff \exists A$ such that $f(x)=Ax$
